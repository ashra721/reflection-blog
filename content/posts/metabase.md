Just after the week I got see something that was extremely new to me. I have never thought of what my working experience will be like using a metabase to design a dashboard . I don’t want to say that is was easy and stuff like that. For me, it felt like a small kid given lots of colour pallets with all kinds of brushes, which if he knew how to use he could get an incredibly beautiful art piece, but the thing is that he dont’t know the skill to hold a brush while painting. Which is one of the most important things to know, even though he had so many ideas to design, but he couldn’t.


Anyways, I tried to explore as much as I could while creating the dashboard. Even though I am not personally satisfied with the little things I got to explore in a short span of time. We were given a set of data on pollution on which we were supposed to come up with different questions and answer them using visualisations such as bar graphs and pie charts. It had a core concept of SQL like grouping and classifying things out. I was a bit familiar with MySQL, but was not able to use enough of its concepts while creating the dashboard. Because I spent a lot of time and effort on different questions. One point where I got stuck for a long was a question I came up with that is how is the pollution level over time? I tried to get it using different filters and summaries, tried everything, Troubleshotted it online, then I realised that the metabase was treating last update as a text, not a date and hence I was not able to do so although I skipped that question for the project, but will definitely explore such things. So yeah, that was about the metabase trek. Now coming back to campus because we have a presentation of our trekking experiences tommorow.
